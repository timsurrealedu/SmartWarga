# Product

## Register

product

## Users

- **Warga (residents)**: Indonesian neighborhood residents, mobile-first (phone as primary device), diverse age range including older adults. Use case: filing reports, checking dues, requesting letters, tracking applications — typically on the go.
- **Pengurus (RT/RW leaders)**: Neighborhood administration leads, more likely on laptop/tablet. Use case: validating residents, approving letters, managing finances, triaging reports — seat-down work at a desk or community post.

## Product Purpose

SmartWarga digitizes Indonesian RT/RW (neighborhood unit) governance — transforming slow paper-based bureaucracy into a responsive digital service. Success = a resident completes an administrative task (surat, dues payment, complaint) in minutes, not days. The platform serves as the single trusted source of truth for a neighborhood's administration, finances, and safety.

## Brand Personality

Warm, Transparent, Empowering — the digital extension of gotong-royong (community cooperation). Feels like a trusted neighbor who happens to know how to code, not a government system or startup dashboard.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS** (pale blue, Notion-style, Linear-gray): Could be any B2B tool; has no cultural grounding.
- **Old Indonesian government portals** (MyGov, e-KTP sites, Dukcapil): Dated blue/white institutional UI; reads as bureaucratic and untrustworthy; exactly what SmartWarga is replacing.

## Design Principles

1. **Disappear into the task** — UI should feel invisible; a warga should complete a surat request without thinking about the interface.
2. **Trust through legibility** — Every number, status, and action must be instantly readable. Contrast is civic duty here; this serves diverse ages.
3. **Warmth without decoration** — The dark-green palette and orange accent carry warmth; that's enough. No gradients, no excessive animation, no flourish for its own sake.
4. **Mobile-first, desktop-worthy** — Design at 375px first; scale up with additive density for admin/pengurus use on larger screens.
5. **Local context, global craft** — Indonesian language and civic norms, but component quality matching the best tools in its category.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG AA for all text and interactive elements.
- Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast; large text ≥ 3:1.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected for all transitions.
- Consider older adult residents: minimum 14px for any actionable text, tap targets ≥ 44×44px on mobile.
